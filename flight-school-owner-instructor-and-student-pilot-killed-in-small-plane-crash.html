Flight School Owner, Instructor And Student Pilot Killed In Small Plane Crash - SarkariResult

News: Fly Lugu Flight School, situated in western Massachusetts, has recently encountered a tragic plane crash leading to the unfortunate loss of three lives. The individuals involved in the incident were Fredrika Ballard, the owner of the flight school, William Hampton, a flight instructor, and Chad Davidson, a student pilot.

Details of the Incident

As per the Massachusetts State Police, the three victims have been identified: Frederika Ballard, a 53-year-old resident of Southwick, Massachusetts; Chad Davidson, a 29-year-old from Woodstock, Connecticut; and William Hampton, a 68-year-old from Indian Orchard, Massachusetts. Ballard was the owner of Fly Lugu Flight School, with Hampton serving as a flight instructor, and Davidson as a student pilot.

The authorities discovered the wreckage and the victims at the crash site, and unfortunately, all three individuals were pronounced dead at the scene. The aircraft involved in the crash was a Beechcraft 55 Baron Twin-Piston, owned by Fly Lugu Flight School. The investigation revealed that the plane took off from Barnes Airport in Westfield, Massachusetts, at approximately 11:06 a.m. on the day of the crash.

The incident was reported by dog walkers in Leyden and Greenfield, who observed a small plane in distress. State police were notified, prompting a search operation. The wreckage was eventually located in a clearing within the Leyden Wildlife Management Area, near the Greenfield-Leyden town line, adjacent to a forested mountain.

Investigation by FAA and NTSB

Following the incident, both the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) initiated an investigation. The precise cause of the crash remains undetermined, and the investigation is focused on uncovering the factors that led to this tragic accident. The FAA and NTSB have confirmed the fatalities of all three individuals involved in the crash.

Local law enforcement, specifically the Greenfield police, issued a public warning to avoid Oak Hill Acres Road, which was in proximity to the crash site. They emphasized that individuals attempting to approach the incident would be turned away to ensure safety.

To sum up, the recent aviation incident with Fly Lugu Flight School in western Massachusetts resulted in the tragic loss of Fredrika Ballard, William Hampton, and Chad Davidson. The FAA and NTSB are actively investigating the incident to ascertain its cause. Our heartfelt condolences extend to the families and friends impacted by this unfortunate event.
