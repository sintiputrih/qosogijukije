Bishop Talbert Swan Biography

Talbert Swan is an American Bishop, pastor, prelate, activist, author, radio host, and the NAACP President. He is a prelate of the Church Of God In Christ (COGIC) and serves as the Bishop of the Nova Scotia Ecclesiastical Jurisdiction in Canada. The Church Of God In Christ has more than 12,000 churches and over 6.5 million members who are mostly African-American.

Talbert Swan Bishop

Talbert was appointed Auxiliary Bishop of the Greater Massachusetts Ecclesiastical Jurisdiction on April 3, 2014, by Charles Edward Blake, Sr. His post was approved by the General Assembly and he was ordained bishop on November 9, 2014, during the 107th Annual Holy Convocation.

Blake named him the third bishop of the Nova Scotia Ecclesiastical Jurisdiction on June 6, 2016. He supervises all member churches in the Nova Scotia province as well as gatherings in Pennsylvania, Massachusetts, and New York. On 13th November 2016. He officially received his apostolic duty during the 109th Annual Holy Convocation.

Talbert Swan Fox News

Swan received racist voicemails after discussing the Covington Catholic video on Fox News. He agreed to go on Fox News to discuss the viral confrontation between Covington Catholic High School students and a Native American demonstrator. He instead received a lot of angry and racist voicemails after his segment aired. Swan posted some of the voicemails and comments on his Facebook page.

Swan said in an interview that he received nearly 150 voicemails within a day of his appearance. Some of the messages were racist and others were more broadly critical of his statements that the high schoolers were jeering Phillips and that their “Make America Great Again” baseball hats are a symbol of racism, according to Swan.

During his appearance on Laura Ingraham’s Fox News show, Swan clashed with Ingraham and guest Dan Bongino over what happened in the Covington Catholic Viral video.
