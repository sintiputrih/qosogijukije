Will Anderson Jr. Bio: Family &amp; Net Worth

Will Anderson Jr. is a rising athlete on his way to establishing a name for himself and carrying out his family’s legacy.

NFL player Will hails from an athletic background, as his father, Will Anderson Sr., is a retired NFL player. So, it is evident where the defensive end’s fierce nature came from.

At the young age of 21, Anderson Jr. has succeeded in being drafted into the NFL, which is worth applauding.

Looking at all his current achievements and honors, it is certain that he will leave a footprint in the NFL.

Family And Siblings

The defensive end was born to Will Anderson Sr. and Tereon Anderson. Anderson Sr. is a former NFL offensive tackle who retired from the games with numerous accolades and honors.

Anderon Sr., 48 years old as of 2023, played for the Cincinnati Bengals from 1996 to 2007. Later, in 2008, he signed a contract with the Baltimore Ravens and played 10 games for the season. 

After that, Anderson Sr. bid a farewell to the NFL for good. Overall, the former athlete played 195 games.

Also, he received many accolades during his career, including the Cincinnati Bengals Ring of Honour, PFWA All-Rookie Team (1996), Pro Bowl (2003-2006), and many more.

After retirement, Anderson Sr. also worked as a firefighter and a paramedic, according to various sources. 

Similarly, Will’s mother, Tereon, served as the teacher. Now, she is an active member of the Hampton Community.

Will Anderson Jr.’s Siblings

Houston’s defensive end has five elder sisters: Teria, Endia, Chyna, Syawnta, and Shanice. Besides his father and mother, Will’s big sisters have been the supportive and pillar of strength in his life.

Will’s sister, Teria, works as a hairstylist, and the second eldest sister, Endia, is a flight attendant.

Likewise, Chyna is a music teacher fond of 90s R&B music. In the same way, Syawnta works as a conversation analyst, and Shanice is a brand designer and owns “The Biz Physician.”

Early Life, Education & Career

Will went to Dutchtown High School, where he learned to play football in the most precise manner. During his time at the Dutchtown, he made several records.

For instance, Will completed his high school career by being named to The Atlanta Journal-Constitution’s Super 11 team. Also, the athlete bagged an opportunity to play in the 2020 All-American Bowl.

Will chose to play college football soon after completing his high school. Among many, he chose to attend the University of Alabama in 2020. There, he received many awards, like the Lombardi Award (2022) and Chuck Bednarik Award (2022), to name a few. 

Will was taught by many of his close ones to play football since childhood to make a great future in the NFL. For instance, the former NFL defensive, Dwight O’Neal Johnson, coached Anderson about technique and hand moves.

Additionally, the retired defensive end Marcus Howard taught Anderson about speed and explosion. Will was also mentored by his uncle Kendall. 

Eventually, all those mentorships paid off when Anderson was selected by the Texans third overall in the 2023 NFL Draft.
