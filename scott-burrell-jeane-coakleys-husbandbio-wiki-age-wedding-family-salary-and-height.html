Scott Burrell Career

Burrell was drafted by another baseball team after his freshman year at University of Connecticut. Burrell alongside with the Toronto Blue Jays decided to spend his summers playing minor league baseball while concentrating on basketball for the rest of the year.

His basketball career would quickly overshadow his baseball career, however, as he became the first player in NCAA history to compile over 1,500 points, 750 rebounds, 275 assists, and 300 steals. He is best known for his full-court pass with 1 second on the clock to Tate George, who hit a shot as time expired to beat Clemson in the East Regional Semi-final in the 1990 NCAA tournament.
