Crystal Gayle is an award-winning American country music singer who was born Brenda Gail Webb on January 9, 1951. She had 20 number one country songs throughout the 1970s and 1980s (18 on Billboard and 2 on Cashbox) and six albums certified Gold by the RIAA. She is best known for her 1977 country-pop crossover smash song “Don’t It Make My Brown Eyes Blue.” With her album “We Must Believe in Magic,” released in 1977, Crystal Gayle became the first female artist in country music history to achieve Platinum sales. She was also named one of the 50 most beautiful persons in the world by People Magazine in 1983, thanks to her almost floor-length hair. She is Loretta Lynn’s youngest sister (by 19 years) and a distant relative of singer Patty Loveless. Crystal Gayle has a star next to Loretta Lynn’s on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
