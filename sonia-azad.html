Sonia Azad- Biography

Sonia Azad is an award-winning American broadcast journalist and reporter for the Dallas, Texas-based television station WFAA-TV, specializing in wellness and health reporting. She also intends to provide ladies with a comprehensive understanding of the perplexing and frequently misunderstood process of egg freezing by sharing her personal experiences through her essays. Sonia has covered numerous big news items in the United States and around the world as a reporter.

What is the Net Worth of Sonia Azad? Salary, Earnings

According to Payscale, the WFAA anchor earns around $90,000. She has an estimated net worth of more than $700,000, which she has amassed through her successful journalism career in just the last decade.

Sonia Azad- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Sonia Azad was born on September 17, 1982, in Houston, Texas, USA. She is of Asian-American heritage and is of part-Persian (Farsi) descent.

As of 2019, she is 37 years old. Sonia’s childhood and how she became interested in the field of journalism are unknown; however, it is known that after graduating from high school, she attended the University of Texas, Austin, where she earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Journalism. She also attended Northwestern University’s Medill School of Journalism and its famed law school, where she earned a Master’s Degree in Journalism and a Master of Legal Studies in 2010.

Sonia Azad- Relationship, Married Life, Boyfriend, Girlfriend

Sonia was supposed to be dating her coworker on News 8 Daybreak, Ron Corning after fans saw their on-screen chemistry. They were close and even exchanged gifts on their birthdays, leading many to speculate that they are either married or siblings. The corporation eventually issued a statement confirming that they were not in a relationship. Sonia tweeted a selfie of herself with a teddy bear in September of 2017 and identified him as her boyfriend.

Sonia is still single in 2019 and spends the majority of her time working, describing herself as a workaholic. When she isn’t traveling the world covering news, she’s either teaching yoga, running a marathon, or doing charity work, which leaves no time for a significant other. Sonia has stated that running a marathon in Antarctica appears to be a more likely option than finding a husband. Despite the fact that she does not have a significant other, Sonia has expressed a desire to have a child or children, with or without a spouse. After her friends had gone through the process, she detailed her 24-day adventure with egg-freezing on her blog in 2016, guaranteeing that she had viable eggs that would enable her to become a mother when the time came.

Sonia Azad- Professional Career

Sonia obtained her first professional experience while studying at the University’s radio station. She worked for local television stations after graduation, and as a result, she lived in cities including San Diego, Waco, Chicago, and College Station. She eventually worked as a reporter for the television station KTRK-TV for six years after accumulating extensive reporting expertise. During this time, she covered international and local stories for WAMU radio station, including war crimes in Africa and the Balkans, the International Criminal Court and the International Criminal Court for the former Yugoslavia in The Hague, Netherlands, and youth and politics in the run-up to the 2008 Presidential election.

Her most notable foreign jobs include covering the Columbia Space Shuttle accident for Star Television from New Delhi, India, and reporting on Afghans and Iranians seeking asylum in France in Paris. Domestically, she has covered the Dallas police ambush in which five officers were killed, as well as former US President Barack Obama’s subsequent visit to Texas, as well as various man-made and natural disasters such as the aftermath of Hurricane Harvey, the mass theater shooting in Aurora, Colorado, the Ebola crisis in Dallas, and the bureaucratic campaign of Texas Senate member Wendy Davis. Sonia was also among the first reporters to report about Houston Mayor Annise Parker’s same-sex marriage.

Sonia was offered a position at ABC-affiliated WFAA television station after five years, and she joined the network in October of 2015. The reporter may be seen on a weekday part of ‘News 8 Daybreak,’ where she discusses her specialties of health, wellness, and lifestyle, with the goal of inspiring others to live their best lives. She has also covered other stories such as presidential elections from various perspectives and multinational projects. Sonia has stated that she occasionally shoots and edits her own stories.

Marathon runner and yoga instructor

Sonia not only runs marathons in and around Texas when she is not covering breaking news, but she also teaches her own yoga courses. She is a certified yoga instructor with over 15 years of expertise in several yoga disciplines from Nicaragua, Mexico, France, and the Netherlands. This encounter began while she was a student at the University of Texas in the dance department, and she has been practicing yoga since then. She is passionate about guiding individuals, from novices to elite athletes, through the process of discovering movement. Her tranquil and gentle Vinyasa flow sessions are designed to heal and awaken the mind and body, with the goal of making the experience as joyful and meaningful for individuals involved as possible.

She believes in applying what she’s learned on the yoga mat to live life with more presence, courage, gratitude, and patience. Yoga, she claims, is crucial for maintaining spiritual balance and coping with the daily rigors of her journalism job.

Sonia’s Volunteer Work

Sonia devotes a significant amount of time outside of her professional duties to making the community a better place. She serves on numerous charitable groups and councils, including the Asian Leadership Council for the Houston Symphony, the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society, the Houston Advisory Council for the Tahirih Justice Center, and the Halo House Foundation Board of Directors. She is frequently spotted delivering Meals on Wheels to older folks in South Dallas.

Achievements and awards

Azad’s hard work and dedication were recognized in 2011 when she received an Emmy Award for her half-hour special report, ‘Passage to India.’ The Texas Associated Press Broadcasters named her one of the state’s best reporters the following year.

Body Measurements: Height, Weight, Hair color

Sonia is Asian, with black hair and dark brown eyes. She stands 5ft 3ins (1.58m) tall and weighs 118lbs (53kgs). She has a polished and elegant appearance and enjoys wearing Indian saris.
