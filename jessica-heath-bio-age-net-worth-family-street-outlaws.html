Jessica Heath On Street Outlaws

Street Outlaws makes changes to its cast every season as it shifts location. Well, season 4 of Street Outlaws: Fastest in America, introduced Team Insane, and Jessica was one of its members.

The last we saw her, Jessica was asked to arm wrestle for lane choice before her race. She was set to race North Carolina on the track. However, she arm wrestled with NOLA team member Kye Kelley first. So, when she ultimately lost, JJ Da Boss instead flipped a coin for the racer’s lane choice before she headed into the car feeling “confident.”

Besides this, Jessica shared that she has raced at least two other times throughout the season — which she expected will make it into the series.

Sadly, her team was near the bottom of the leaderboard then. Nonetheless, she managed to make a substantial impression on viewers

Jessica’s Team Insane was just one of the new teams that joined the show for its fourth installment. The new teams were 252 Outlaws and Trailer Park Mafia.

As for the returning teams, they were Dream Team, Nola, Memphis, Detroit, and Texas.

Jessica Heath Job

Jessica Heath is a street racer and bodybuilder. So far, she’s competed in 13 shows — started in 2014, and still going strong.

She won her pro card in Pittsburgh during her 3rd national show.

Sadly, her injury has sidelined her career and has forced her to be more of a coach. But luckily, she’s had the chance to coach some very successful athletes.

In 2023, she worked at Metroflex Gym in Oceanside, founded by Christians Gavin Louis Uridel and Josh Barnett. Also, she owned the Outdoor Gym in San Clemente named FitLab. There, she offered services such as Meal planning, Personal Training, Group sessions, Competition Prep, and Posing.

Find FitLab on IG @fitlab_by_inkedjessy.

Jessica Heath Family

Jessica Heath comes from a family of five.

Her father, David C Heath hails from Maysville, North Carolina, and is a motor fanatic as well. “Happy Father’s Day to the best dad in the entire world! I love you, Dad!  Thank you for being who you are. You always treated me the way a daughter is supposed to be treated. You take pride in the little things. And I am happy to carry on traits from you. You have always been an amazing father and I cherish how close we are and always will be. ❤️ now let’s go racing,” Jessica dedicated an FB post to him in June 2020.

As for her mother, Brenda Heath, Jessica said that she was the “best and strongest mom” she knew. “I can truly say I have a mom who is truly a great mom and always put us first,” she added.

David and Brenda were 59 and 58 years of age respectively in 2023.

David is a White Oak High and Hunters Creek Middle graduate. He lived in  Maysville, North Carolina, and was a racer as well.

Stephanie lived in Lewisville, North Carolina with her husband Roger Mock III, and their two kids.
