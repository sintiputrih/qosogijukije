Lisa Lopes’ Biography

Lisa Lopes, known by her stage name “Left Eye,” was a Grammy Award-winning hip hop artist. Born on March 27, 1971, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, she gained fame as a member of the girl group TLC. Lopes’ career spanned from 1990 until her untimely death in a vehicular accident in 2002.

Lisa Lopes was the daughter of Ronald Lopes Sr., a US Army staff sergeant, and Wanda, a seamstress. Raised alongside her brother Ronald and younger sister Reigndrop, Lisa cultivated her passion for music by performing gospel songs with her siblings as The Lopes Kids at local events and churches. After attending Philadelphia High School for Girls, she moved to Atlanta, Georgia to audition for a new girl group.

The group initially named 2nd Nature was later rebranded as TLC, incorporating the initials of Tionne, Lisa, and Crystal. TLC’s debut album “Ooooooohhh… On the TLC Tip” released in 1992 achieved multi-platinum status with over four million copies sold in the US alone. Their subsequent album “CrazySexyCool” was even more successful, selling over 11 million copies in the US and earning multiple Grammy Awards.

In 1999, TLC recorded their third studio album called “FanMail,” which achieved 6x platinum status and topped various music charts. Even posthumously, Lisa Lopes’ impact in the music industry continued with the release of “Supernova” in 2001 and “Eye Legacy” in 2009, featuring collaborations with renowned artists.

On a personal note, Lisa Lopes endured challenges, including legal troubles and a tragic car accident in Honduras which claimed her life. Her legacy lives on through her music, with her solo verse from the hit song “Waterfalls” engraved on her tombstone.
