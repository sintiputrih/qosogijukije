Lamar Jackson – Professional Life

On January 5, 2018, Jackson entered the 2018 NFL Draft. He was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in the first round with the 32nd overall pick in the draft. Similarly, on 18th November, he made his first NFL start against the Cincinnati Bengals. He went 13 for 19 for 150 yards and an interception and rushed for 117 yards.

Jackson became the youngest quarterback to ever start a post-season game. He finished the game by completing 14 of 29 passes for 194 yards, the two aforementioned touchdowns, and an interception while rushing for 54 yards, losing one fumble.

In the 2019 season against the Cincinnati Bengals, he finished with 236 passing yards and 152 rushing yards. He has played eight games where his 637 rushing yards led Michael Vick’s 2006 record of 573 for most by a quarterback. His 95.4 passer rating was also the highest among Ravens quarterbacks at midseason.

In the ninth game of the season, the Ravens routed the Bengals. He became only the second player in NFL history to produce two perfect passer ratings in the same season.

In February 2020, he became one of 40 of the most inspiring and innovative vanguards in African-American culture.
