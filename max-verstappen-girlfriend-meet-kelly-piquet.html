Biography of Max Verstappen

Max Emilian Verstappen is a Dutch racing driver and two-time Formula One World Champion. He was born on September 30, 1997, in Hasselt, Belgium, to former Formula One driver Jos Verstappen and Belgian karting champion Sophie Kumpen.

Verstappen began his motorsport career in karting, following in the footsteps of his mother and father. He quickly rose through the ranks and won several championships, including the 2013 World KZ Championship, before making the jump to single-seaters.

In 2014, at just 16 years old, Verstappen joined the FIA European Formula 3 Championship with Van Amersfoort Racing. He immediately showed his potential by winning his first race in the series and finished third in the championship standings in his rookie year.

The following year, Verstappen made his Formula One debut with Scuderia Toro Rosso, becoming the youngest driver to ever compete in the series at just 17 years old. He impressed with his speed and skill, finishing in the points on multiple occasions, and was quickly signed by Red Bull Racing for the 2016 season.

Verstappen’s time at Red Bull Racing has been marked by his incredible speed and fearless driving style. He has won numerous races, including the 2019 Austrian Grand Prix, where he famously overtook Charles Leclerc on the final lap to take the victory.

In 2021, Verstappen finally fulfilled his long-awaited dream of becoming the Formula One World Champion. He battled fiercely with seven-time world champion Lewis Hamilton throughout the season, ultimately coming out on top with a dramatic victory at the season finale in Abu Dhabi.

Verstappen successfully defended his title in 2022, winning eight races and securing the championship with four races still remaining in the season.

Outside of his racing career, Verstappen is known for his laid-back personality and his love of extreme sports such as snowboarding and motocross. He is also a keen sim racer and has competed in several esports events.
