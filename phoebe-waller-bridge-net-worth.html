What is Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Net Worth and Salary?

Phoebe Waller-Bridge, an English actress and writer, has a net worth of $40 million. She created and starred in the hit sitcom “Fleabag,” which received widespread critical praise. Additionally, she was the showrunner, head writer, and executive producer of “Killing Eve,” which also garnered critical acclaim. Waller-Bridge is now considered one of the most influential figures in the entertainment industry.

Aside from her television work, Waller-Bridge has acted in series like “The Cafe” and “Broadchurch,” and has appeared in films such as “Albert Nobbs,” “The Iron Lady,” “Goodbye Christopher Robin,” and “Solo: A Star Wars Story.” She also helped write the screenplay for the James Bond movie “No Time to Die.”

For her work on “Fleabag,” Waller-Bridge won several awards, including a British Academy Television Award, three Emmy Awards, and two Golden Globe Awards. She avoids social media.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Lucrative Development Deal with Amazon

Phoebe Waller-Bridge signed a $20 million per year development deal with Amazon Studios in 2019 to create and produce content for Amazon Prime. However, according to insiders, she did not produce any shows during the first three years of the deal (2020-2022). Despite this, she re-signed an identical 3-year $60 million extension, bringing her total earnings to $120 million.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Background

Phoebe Mary Waller-Bridge, born in 1985, grew up in West London in a highly aristocratic family. Her father, Michael Cyprian Waller-Bridge, founded Tradepoint, while her mother, Theresa Mary, is related to the Worshipful Company of Ironmongers. Phoebe’s family has a history of serving as soldiers and clergymen and became landed gentry in Sussex. She attended an all-girls Catholic School after her parents divorced and later studied acting at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art. Although she was religious during her childhood, Phoebe has since become an atheist.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Career Highlights

Phoebe Waller-Bridge began her career in theatre, co-founding her own theatre company, DryWrite Theatre Company, in 2007. She gained credits for her roles in plays like “Roaring Trade,” “Rope,” “Hayfever,” and “Mydidae.” In 2012, she wrote the play that eventually became the sitcom “Fleabag,” which premiered in 2016. She also wrote and starred in the sitcom “Crashing.”

Waller-Bridge turned her attention towards film and television in 2009, guest-starring in TV sitcoms and dramas and booking roles in films like “The Night Watch” and “The Iron Lady.” She established herself as a voice actress and wrote and produced series like “Killing Eve” and “Run.” In 2019, she wrote the screenplay for the Bond film “No Time to Die.”

Waller-Bridge’s work on “Fleabag” earned her numerous awards, honors, and accolades. She also played droid L3-37 in “Solo: A Star Wars Story” in 2018, which received mixed reviews and resulted in losses for Disney.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Relationship History

Phoebe Waller-Bridge married Conor Woodman, an Irish filmmaker and presenter, in 2014. However, the relationship ended in divorce by 2017. One year later, she began a relationship with Martin McDonagh, a playwright.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Voiceover Roles in Advertisements

Phoebe Waller-Bridge has taken on voiceover roles in several TV advertisements to supplement her acting income. She has lent her voice to brands such as Gordon’s Gin, Tropicana, and Travel Republic, among others.
